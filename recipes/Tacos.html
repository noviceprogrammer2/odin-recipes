Tacos

Description

If I had to eat three foods for the rest of my life, tacos would be on that list. They're delicious, versatile, and easy to make. For this recipe, we will be cooking classic tacos with ground beef, tomatoes, lettuce, cheese, and some salsa! As TacoBell says, Live Mas!

Side Dishes

Some possible side dishes for this meal include Goya yellow rice and refried beans.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of 80/20 ground beef
  • 1 flavor packet of Old El Paso Low Sodium Taco Seasoning
  • 1 cup of Old El Paso Thick 'n Chunky salsa
  • 10 pack of Taco shells
  • 1/2 head of lettuce, shredded
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped (3/4 cup)
  • 1 cup shredded Cheddar Cheese (4ounces)

Steps

  1. Drizzle Canola Oil in a large pot coating the bottom in a thin layer. Heat oil for a few minutes and then add ground beef. Cook beef for 8 to 10 minutes (until fully browned) while stirring occasionally. Once done, drain fat.
  2. Add pot of beef back onto the stove on medium heat. Add half cup of water to beef and taco seasoning. Stir till beef is coated. It will look dark brown. Add Garlic powder, onion powder salt, and lime to taste. Stir Salsa into beef aswell. Cook till mixture is hot but not burning.
  3. Follow Instructions on the taco shell package for cooking. Make sure to watch shells so they do not burn.
  4. Serve Taco Shells with lettuce, diced tomato, cheese, and ground beef.
